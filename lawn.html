There’s such a trade-off with age. You see so many things diminish or change in your physical abilities. You can’t burn off calories without throwing your back out. But it’s also fun going to Starbucks at 7 am without giving a #$@ about your bed hair and mismatched clothes. For all of my youth, I needed music as a soundtrack to everything I was doing. Now silence and bird chirps are preferred. As a Baby Boomer, all the Classic Rock just plays at will on a mental juke box in my head anyway.

As a kid, I was exposed to a lot of older relatives from sleepy old towns in Delaware and the Eastern Shore of Maryland. Those funny- smelling old relatives had features that now stare back at me from the bathroom mirror. And now I scour Ancestry.com for clues about ancestors, when I could have just asked them when they were alive. But as Dylan said: “I was so much older then, I’m younger than that now.”
