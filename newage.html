From my earliest days, I was fascinated with everything paranormal, occult, metaphysical, sacred, esoteric, mystical, and extraterrestrial. The more that people scoffed at those subjects, the more stubborn I got about defending the right to poke and stretch our belief systems. When Out on a Limb helped boost the popularity of the New Age Movement in the early 80s, I was on that bus. My approach was to read everything and try everything. A lot of that woo-woo stuff looked silly and easy to make fun of. I always came to the defense of the people with open minds and adventurous spirits and shot the bird to all the skeptics who thought they had a better understanding of how the universe works. I can laugh at how silly some of it was, but at least I experimented before making my conclusions. I’ve experienced ghosts and seen a few UFOs or at least that’s my story. I tried LSD a few times and enjoyed the view. I became interested in crop circles and that led me to my interest in Earth Mysteries, standing stones, and sacred sites. I spent four years making a crop circle documentary called CircleSpeak. I had more than my share of awesome cosmic memories.
