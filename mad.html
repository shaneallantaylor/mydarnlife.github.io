My Darn Life

I’m a 1956 Baby Boomer. This was a perfect vintage to enjoy Mad Magazine, Marvel Comics, and (later) Nixon-era National Lampoon and Rolling Stone. I was fueled by Coca Cola, candy, stick pretzels, and Lance Crackers. I had older brothers who brought Dylan, Beatles and Hendrix albums into the house. TV went from B&W to color and it was awesome! Rat Patrol. Outer Limits. Man From U.N.C.L.E. We weren’t the Greatest Generation, but I contend that Baby Boomers were the greatest Pop Culture and Network News generation – we stood outside and looked at the moon while men walked on it. I heard Penny Lane and Sittin’ on the Dock of the Bay on the transistor radio tuned to WSBA in York, Pennsylvania. My heroes were fighter pilots and third basemen and then playboy-spies, and then lead guitar players. I always kept my eyes peeled for flying saucers and I think they had their eyes on me as well. Childhood was awesome.
