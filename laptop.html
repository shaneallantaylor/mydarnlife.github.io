During the 1980s, I wrote sketch comedy and parodies, and produced a few comedy videos. This was back in the day when VHS cameras were as big and heavy as Sears Craftsman toolboxes. I had to rent an editing suite. During the 1990s I took television writing classes in D.C. and began to get the hang of it. I moved to Los Angeles just as sit coms hit the skid, so I shifted my creative focus to feature-length screenwriting. One comedy screenplay, “Gunfighters from Mars” got me an agent and two studio meetings. Another comedy, “Hammerheads”, won first place in a national contest and brought me a scholarship to a 2- year screenwriting program. Meanwhile, I kept My Darn Job, and also spent about four years producing and marketing a feature-length documentary called “CircleSpeak”. My production partner moved to New Zealand for a few years, but after his return we made some comedy videos that can be found on Funny or Die and You Tube. The process I went through all seemed pretty Neolithic when nowadays you can write scripts on your IPAD, film sketches on your IPhone, and send them out to the world via You Tube. Along the way, I gained tremendous respect for the craft of screenwriting, even if I never had a script sale or option. I think storytelling is important in more ways than we can imagine.
